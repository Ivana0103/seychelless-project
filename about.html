About Seychelles

Welcome to Seychelles

Seychelles is an archipelago of 115 islands located in the Indian Ocean.
Known for its breathtaking beaches, vibrant culture, and unique biodiversity, Seychelles is a popular tourist destination.

Geography and Climate

Seychelles is situated around 1,600 km east of mainland Africa, in the Indian Ocean. The archipelago consists of both granite and coral islands, each offering distinct landscapes. The granite islands are home to lush tropical forests, while the coral islands feature white-sand beaches and clear, turquoise waters.

The climate is tropical, with warm temperatures throughout the year. The wet season occurs from November to March, while the dry season lasts from May to September, making Seychelles a perfect year-round destination for travelers seeking a tropical escape.

A Haven for Wildlife

Seychelles is renowned for its unique biodiversity. The islands are home to over 2,000 species of plants and animals, many of which are found nowhere else in the world. Among the most famous species are the giant Aldabra tortoises, which can be found on the Aldabra Atoll, and the black parrot, the national bird of Seychelles.

Endless Adventures

Whether you're an adrenaline junkie or simply looking to relax, Seychelles has something for everyone. You can indulge in various activities such as:

  • Snorkeling and Scuba Diving: Explore vibrant coral reefs, swim alongside tropical fish, and discover the underwater beauty of Seychelles.
  • Island Hopping: Visit the many different islands that make up the archipelago, each with its unique charm and beauty.
  • Hiking: Hike through tropical forests, ascend granite peaks, and enjoy stunning panoramic views of the islands.
  • Beach Relaxation: Enjoy the tranquil, powdery white-sand beaches and crystal-clear waters for a relaxing experience.

Conservation Efforts

Seychelles is committed to preserving its natural beauty for future generations. The country has implemented several conservation programs to protect its unique ecosystems. Over 50% of the land area is designated as protected areas, including national parks and nature reserves.

The government and local organizations are also working on marine conservation, focusing on coral reefs and the protection of endangered species such as the hawksbill turtle and the whale shark. Visitors are encouraged to respect local wildlife and contribute to sustainable tourism efforts.
